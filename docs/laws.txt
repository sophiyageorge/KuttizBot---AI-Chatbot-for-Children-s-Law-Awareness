Title: What are my rights?
You have the right to go to school, be safe, play, and be treated kindly. 

Title: What if someone hurts me?
You can talk to a teacher or parent. There are laws that protect you.

Title: Who can help me?
A teacher, parent, or child helpline. You can also dial 1098.

Title: What is good touch and bad touch?
A good touch makes you feel happy and safe. A bad touch makes you feel scared or confused. Always tell someone if you feel scared.

2. Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 (POCSO)
What it does: Protects children (<18) from sexual abuse, harassment, and pornography.

Key Features:

Child-friendly court procedures.

Mandatory reporting of offences.

Covers both boys and girls.

What it does: Prohibits employment of children below 14 years in all occupations and hazardous processes.

5. The Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006
What it does: Prohibits marriage of boys under 21 and girls under 18.

Key Features:

Child marriages are voidable at the option of the minor.

Provides for punishment of people promoting or performing child marriages.

6. The Commissions for Protection of Child Rights Act, 2005
What it does: Provides for the creation of the National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) and State Commissions.

Key Features:

Monitors implementation of child-related laws.

Can inquire into complaints about violations of child rights.

7. The Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956
What it does: Prevents trafficking of children for prostitution or immoral purposes.

Key Features:

Protects victims of trafficking.

Punishes traffickers and those aiding them.

8. Information Technology Act, 2000 (Relevant to Cyber Safety of Children)
What it does: Addresses issues of cyberbullying, online abuse, and child pornography.

Key Features:

Punishment for publishing or transmitting obscene material involving children.

Section 67B deals with child exploitation online.

9. The Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act, 1971 (for minors)
What it does: Requires parental or guardian consent if the pregnant person is below 18.
